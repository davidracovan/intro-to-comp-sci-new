Joseph R. Biden Jr., 46th President of the USA

Aspirations

President Biden has pledged to beat COVID-19, create millions of well-paying jobs and build back the economy, build on the Affordable Care Act, tackle climate change, and unite the union.

Joe Biden's Achievements

  • 1986: Proposed one of the first climate change bills in the Senate.
  • 1986: Spoke about the need to place sanctions on South Africa.
  • 1987-1995: Served as chair of the Judiciary Committee.
  • 1990s: Advocated for intervention in Yugoslavia.
  • 1994: Co-sponsored the Violence Against Women Act in 1994.
  • 1994: Defended the 1994 Crime Bill, one of the most controversial aspects of Biden's career.
  • 2008: Elected as Vice President.
  • 2008: Passed the PROTECT Our Children Act.
  • 2010: Role in the passage of the Affordable Care Act.
  • 2010: Helped the Obama Administration pass the 2010 Relief Act.
